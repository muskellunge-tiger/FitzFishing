Strategies

When it came to fishing East Canyon in 2018 it seemed to be relatively slow. But in the early spring when fishing from shore the best method seemed to be fishing on the bottom with half a worm and half a marshmallow. We used a standard sized water-bubble to get the bait to the bottom. If you don't know what a water-bubble is please read the paragraph below. The reason we put half a marshmallow on is to float the worm off the bottom, so the crawdads will have a harder time eating your bait allowing for you to have a much better chance to catch a fish. Other shore fishing lures that worked were 2" white tub jigs, which worked best in the winter, and spinners and spoons. It was a spoon that caught our biggest fish out of East Canyon which was a 21" Brown Trout.

When fishing from a boat the best gear to use was pop-gear and a worm. We trolled our pop-gear at around 1-1.5mph. The best pop-gear was five bladed silver pop-gear which is what caught most of the fish on our one great day we had at East Canyon where we caught 25 fish. If you are planning on fishing from a boat in the summer months I recommend you get out early cause the amount recreational boaters can get pretty bad and can cause the fish not to bite. For the one Wiper we caught we where using a 3" white crankbait, and casting it in close to shore and reeling it back at a decent speed.

What is a Water-bubble?

A water-bubble is a round oval like clear bubble that you fill with water to allow baits to sink to the bottom. Now the reason I use this over snap weights is because the water-bubble allows the line to slide through it, so the fish won't feel any tension when it takes your bait. This will cause the fish to hold and eat your bait for longer allowing you to get a better hook set. It will also allow you to get a longer cast over snap weights.
