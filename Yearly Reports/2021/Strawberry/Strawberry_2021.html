Strategies

Overview

Strawberry in this year was just like it is every year and that excellent fishing pretty much year round, but especially in the summer and fall. And just like always there was one lure that destroyed all other and that being the white tube jig with in our opinion is the best lure that can ever be used up there. This year was by far our best year so far at Strawberry with many of the days in the summer and fall surpassing 100 fish caught in a single day.

Summer

Best Lures

  • Casting, Jigging, and Drift Trolling 2.5 to 3" White Tube Jigs

Jig Casting Tips

  • We always tipped our jigs with Chub meat for extra scent and flavor to draw fish in.
  • The most common retrieve that we would use for the jigs would be a porpusing retrieve where you would cast out your jig, let it sink, and then begin jigging your rod up and then reeling back down and repeating the process for the entire cast.
  • The way you'll know if you get a hit is when you go to jig it will feel a little bit more heavy than normal and this is because the Trout will normally just grab the jig as it sinks after the jig so you usually don't feel the hit.
  • If you feel a hit then you need to either quickly set the hook or reel down very quickly and then set the hook so the fish doesn't have time to let go of the jig before you can hook them.
  • If you set the hook and miss, don't give up. Reel up some of the slack and let the jig sink for a few seconds, depending on how deep of water you're in, and watch to see if you line either doesn't sink or slacks off as it's sinking. If either of these happen, reel up the remaining slack and set the hook because it means that the fish has hit again.
  • Once your jig comes back into view, watch closly what's going on because sometimes that Trout will follow the jig all the way in. So, if you see a fish following your jig and it hasn't spooked off yet but you're running out of distance to the boat, let your jig sink so that the fish doesn't see the boat and then jig try to jig and get the to bite that way.
  • If you get to the end of your cast and fish is following and you miss it or it turns away on the jig. Don't give up on that fish, watch which direction it swims off too and then flip your jig out in that direction and let it sink for a few seconds and usually the fish will come back and hit it or chase it again.
  • For a visual way of seeing how this casting method should be done please watch the video at the bottom of the page.

Drift Trolling Tips

  • Usually wind picks up at Strawberry in the afternoon and this can make it hard to use the casting method while the boat is moving because your jig will come up faster in the water because of the boats movement. This means that drift trolling tube jigs is the best way to go.
  • When drift trolling you will want to use your fish finder to see where the fish are in the water and where your jig is when you drop it down.
  • What you want to do is drop your jig off the side of the boat where the wind is to your back, so your jig should go under the boat, this is because your jig will drop faster and will show up on your fish finder.
  • Once your jig shows up on the fish finder and you have it dropped to the depth you want move to the other side of the boat so your line is no longer going under the boat so you can begin drift trolling. Usually if you drop right on a fish they will hit the jig right away.
  • Once you are on the other side, for a couple minutes let your jig troll and jig it every now and then to try to attract fish. If you get hit and miss, don't give up, the fish will likely hit again once your jig sinks back down.
  • If you go a little bit without getting hit then bring your jig back using the same method from the casting section above. This will sometimes trigger a bite. If you do not get hit, then simply drop back down repeat the process.

Fall

Best Lures

  • 2.5 to 3" White Tube Jigs
  • Orange and Gold Kamloopers
  • Silver and Gold Pot-O-Golds
  • Apex 5 of Diamonds
  • Lucky Craft 115s

Fall Tips

  • For tube jigs, use the same tips from above. You just usually will not have to let the jig sink as far because the fish are shallower.
  • For the Lucky Crafts, a quick stop and go retrieve or trolling at about 2 to 3MPH. works best.

Ice Fishing

Best Lures

  • 2.5 to 3" White Tube Jigs
  • Yellow Atomic Tubes
  • 2" Green and White Tube Jigs
  • 2" Green Marabou's
  • 1" White Beetle Jigs

Tips

  • We always tipped our bigger jigs with Chub meat and our smaller jigs with wax worms or pink maggots for extra scent and flavor to draw fish in.
  • We will also usually keep our smaller jigs at the top of the water column, about 15' or less, so that higher running fish can still be caught. While our lager jigs are usually kept deeper.
  • A lot of ice fishing is looking at the fish finder and reading what the fish are doing and how they are acting around your lure and what you do with it. Somtimes some fish will want it moving a lot and other times they don't want it moving at all, and there really isn't a way of telling which it'll be most of the time, so you will just have to experiment until you figure out which they like more.
  • If you do have a fish that is aggresive and is wants the jig moving there are some things you can do to trigger a bite. The first is to jig your jig a few times and then let it hang for a second to trigger a strike. The other is to reel up really quick one or two turns and see if the fish follows it in order to get a strike. You will know if the fish like these by the fact that they will follow the jig. And you might have to try each of these methods a few times before getting a bite, so don't give up.
