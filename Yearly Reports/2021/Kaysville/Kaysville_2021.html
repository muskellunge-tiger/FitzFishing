Strategies

Overview

Fishing at Kaysville this year wasn't great but also not bad. Pretty much everytime we went we caught a decent amount of fish. Bottom fishing was by far to best method for the year at Kaysville, followed by tube jigs. It always seemed to be that going on calm very sunny days was the best way to have the best chance at catching the most fish as opposed to cloudy days.

Best Lures and Methods

  • Bottom Fishing with Worms
  • Slowly Retrieving 1" Green Tube Jigs, Yellow Atomic Tubes, and White Beetle Jigs
  • Bobber and a Worm

Bottom Fishing Tips

  • The bait we always used for bottom fishing was 1/3 to 1/2 of a worm and half a marshamllow to float the worm off the bottom on about a 1.5' leader.
  • The sinker we used for the bait was a water-bubble and we like to use these because you can cast farther and the line can slide through the bubble so that the fish will not feel the weight or tension of the gear.
  • When bottom fishing, keeping your bait in the water for a good period of time and having patients is key. Most of the time we say to keep your bait in the water for at least 15 to 20 minutes before bringing it in if you don't get a bite.
  • This is also the best and only method that will work consistanly to catch Catfish and sometimes Wipers in the summer.

Slow Retrieving Tips

  • We always tipped our jigs with pink maggots for extra scent and flavor to draw fish in.
  • Whenever using tube jigs at Kaysville it is best to use a very slow retrieve because of how dirty the water is so that the fish have a chance to see your jig.
  • We also recommend that you use a lighter action rod like light or ultralight, and this is just to make feeling hits and catching fish a lot easier because you are using small jigs and reeling very slow so a heavy rod is not needed.
