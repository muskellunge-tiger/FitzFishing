Strategies

When fishing Jensen in mid to late winter this year, there were two methods that worked very well. They were: bottom fishing and fishing with tube jigs or Marabou's. When bottom fishing, there was a lot of different baits that would work well. The best of them for us was either half a worm and half a marshmallow or rainbow pattered Trout nuggets. The other baits that would work were just a whole marshmallow or chartreuse green powerbait. One thing we realized when fishing with the Trout nuggets was that it was better to cut the nugget in half. The reason this was a good idea was because the fish in Jensen at the time were fairly small, so by cutting the nugget in half, it made a smaller presentation and made it easier for the fish to eat the bait. Sometimes the same would go for the half a worm and marshmallow. If your worms were really big then it would usually be best to cut the worm into quarters instead of in half to give it a small look. The leader length that we used was 6", but if wanted to go as long as 12 to 18" then that would likely work too, and the weight we used was a standard size water bubble. If you don't know what a water bubble is, please read the paragraph below.

Usually if you are fishing on the bottom with bait in a place that has a mostly clean bottom, then bottom bouncing jigs can also be a good way to catch fish, and that was the case at Jensen this year. Because the fish were smaller, bottom bouncing smaller jigs was best. The two best jigs for us were 1 to 1.5" yellow tube jigs or green and white Marabou's. We would always tip our jigs with either pink maggots or fire corn which is corn that is pink. Sometimes, if you were lucky, spinners and spoons would work. But this was very rare because it was still too cold at that time for them to work. But the color that worked best for us was silver.

What is a Water-bubble?

A water-bubble is a round oval like clear bubble that you fill with water to allow baits to sink to the bottom. Now the reason I use this over snap weights is because the water-bubble allows the line to slide through it, so the fish won't feel any tension when it takes your bait. This will cause the fish to hold and eat your bait for longer allowing you to get a better hook set. It will also allow you to get a longer cast over snap weights.
