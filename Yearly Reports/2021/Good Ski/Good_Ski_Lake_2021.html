Strategies

Overview

We did not fish Good Ski Lake that much this year, and the few times that we did the fishing was far from good which. Which was interesting because in previous years it had been a decently good place to fish. Most of the problems that we ran into while fishing there in the fall was the fact that there was a lot more grass near the surface this year as opposed to other years, which made fishing many of lures that we normally had success on very difficult.

Best Methods

  • Bottom Bouncing a 2" Orange or Yellow Tube Jig
  • Bottom fishing with Worm and a Marshmallow

Bottom Bouncing Tips

  • We always tipped our jigs with pink maggots for extra scent and flavor to draw fish in.
  • When bottom bouncing always keep some slack in your line when the jig hits the bottom because the fish will usually need sometime to fully take the jig before you can successfully hook them.
  • Go slow, don't just jig your jig off the bottom right when it hits. Leave it on the bottom for a few seconds so that any fish in the area have time to investigate.
  • Be patient, once the fish bites you will see your line start to pull away, but this does not mean that you should set the hook right away because the fish could drop the jig or not quite have it in it's mouth fully yet. Instead, wait until the fish pulls out almost all the slack in your line before setting the hook.
  • Don't get frustrated. This is a finess method that requires good patients and timing. This means that you will miss some fish, especially if you are new to this kind of fishing method. But if you keep at it, over time you will get better and better.

Bottom Fishing Tips

  • The bait we always used for bottom fishing was 1/4 to 1/2 of a worm and half a marshamllow to float the worm off the bottom on about a 1.5' leader.
  • The sinker we used for the bait was a water-bubble and we like to use these because you can cast farther and the line can slide through the bubble so that the fish will not feel the weight or tension of the gear.
  • When bottom fishing, keeping your bait in the water for a good period of time and having patients is key. Most of the time we say to keep your bait in the water for at least 15 to 20 minutes before bringing it in if you don't get a bite.
