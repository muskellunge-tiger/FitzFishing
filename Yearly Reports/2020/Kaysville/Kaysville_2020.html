Strategies

When fishing Kaysville in the early part of the year. Tube jigs were the dominate lure, like they always seem to be. The best tube jig for us was a small yellow Atomic tube. One thing that made it obvious that this jig was the best choice was the fact that you could fish it without tipping it with anything and still catch fish which is very rare for a tube jig. However, this was not always the case, so if we were fishing it without any bait on it and weren't getting any bites. Then we would tip it with a pink maggot. The retrieve method that worked best for this jig was a slow retrieve. The best way to do this slow retrieve effectively would be to use a lighter action rod. This would be good for a few reasons. The first, is the fact that the fish are smaller, so having a light rod while fishing with a light jig makes it easier to feel bites. Second, sometimes if you have a light rod, when the fish would hit you wouldn't even have to set the hook cause the rod bends so easy that it just sets the hook for you. Whereas a heavier action rod could sometimes rip the jig out of the fish's mouth. Now, this doesn't mean that you can't use a heavier rod when using this method, it just tends to work better with a lighter action rod. If you do plan on using a heavier action rod we recommend that you don't go any heavier than medium action.

Another thing that worked when fishing with the small tube jig was to just cast it out and let it sink to the bottom and just let it sit there. The only time this would work would be if there was a lot of fish in a small area. The way we found this out was by using a larger tube jig and bottom bouncing it, and if we were getting hit in about the same spot every time then we would know that there was a lot of fish in that one area. But because we were using a larger jig, we wouldn't catch many, so we would cast the smaller jig into that area and let sit on the bottom, and most of the time we would get hit shortly after the jig hit the bottom. The only problem with this method is that you have to be patient when a fish first bites because you can't set the hook right away. What you have to do is wait for the fish to pull out most of the slack in your line, then set the hook.

One thing to keep in mind when letting the jig sit on the bottom is that this is not bottom fishing with bait where letting your bait sit on the bottom for 10 to 20 minutes is a good idea. If you cast a tube jig into a good area with aggressive fish, you most of the time will get hit within a minute at most. So, if you don't get hit within a minute or two, you're probably not going to get hit. If this does happen, simply just lift the jig off the bottom, and begin to slowly retrieve it back and sometimes you will get hit while bringing it in.

Another method that worked would be bottom fishing with bait. The best bait for us was half a worm and half a marshmallow on about a 6 to 12" leader with a standard size water bubble as the weight. If you don't know what a water bubble is, please read the paragraph below. Most of the time when we would bottom fish, we would also fish the tube jig as well to maximize our catching. If you plan on doing this there are two things you want to keep in mind. The first is that if the bite is on, it might get a little crazy cause you may have both of your rods get hit at the same time. The second is that you wouldn't want to cast your bottom bait in the same areas you would be casting your jig. For example, you will be able to cast your bottom bait a lot farther than your tube jig, so unless you know that all the fish are in close, you don't want to cast your bottom bait close cause you can work those areas with your jig. The better thing to do is cast your bottom bait in a completely different area that your tube jig can't get too. By doing this, you can effectively fish two different areas at once, and this is good cause then you won't fish out an area as fast and will usually catch more fish. Now, if you are casting bottom bait in a different area than your tube jig and aren't getting any hits then it's okay to cast your bottom bait in the same areas as your tube jig as long as it's not going to interfere with your tube jig.

What is a Water-bubble?

A water-bubble is a round oval like clear bubble that you fill with water to allow baits to sink to the bottom. Now the reason I use this over snap weights is because the water-bubble allows the line to slide through it, so the fish won't feel any tension when it takes your bait. This will cause the fish to hold and eat your bait for longer allowing you to get a better hook set. It will also allow you to get a longer cast over snap weights.
