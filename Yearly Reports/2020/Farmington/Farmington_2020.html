Strategies

This year was the first time we fished Farmington Pond, and one thing that we noticed right away is that the water is almost always very clear. Because of this, fall fishing with spinners and spoons would be red hot at times. The one day that we did extremely well in October we used spinners and spoons for around 3 hours and were able to catch over 40 fish. The best spoons to use were either red white and gold or orange and silver junior Kamloopers and small copper Daredevls. Spinners like #2 silver and green Bluefox's and lil silver Jakes would also work, but not near as good as the spoons. Of the three spoons that worked very well we would say the best one was the red white and gold junior Kamlooper.

While the spinners and spoons work extremely well, they are not always the best option. For example, in the spring or in late fall when it's starting to get colder spinners and spoons don't work as well. This is when bottom fishing works best. For us, the best bottom bait was half a worm and half a marshmallow. Sometimes chartreuse powerbait or a whole marshmallow alone would work too. All the baits that we fished were on a 6 to 12" leader with a standard size water bubble as the weight. If you don't know what a water bubble is, please read the paragraph below. While bottom fishing does work, we found that it was a very hit or miss method to use.

What is a Water-bubble?

A water-bubble is a round oval like clear bubble that you fill with water to allow baits to sink to the bottom. Now the reason I use this over snap weights is because the water-bubble allows the line to slide through it, so the fish won't feel any tension when it takes your bait. This will cause the fish to hold and eat your bait for longer allowing you to get a better hook set. It will also allow you to get a longer cast over snap weights.
