Strategies

Fishing Jensen shortly after the ice melted produced some decent fishing. The first time we went out there was still quite a bit of ice, so there was limited fishing spots and room to cast. This made it harder to catch fish. Once the ice completely melted the fishing got a bit better. While casting small tube jigs and slowing reeling them back worked the best method to use was bottom fishing. The two best baits to use where rainbow patterned powerbait and half a marshmallow with one to two pieces of corn depending on the size (Fishing with corn is now legal everywhere in Utah as of 2019). You would want your bait to be on around a one-foot leader but you could longer or shorter if you wanted. You would also want your gear to be set on a standard sized water-bubble. If you don't know what a water-bubble is please read the paragraph below.

During this time of year fishing on the bottom was pretty much the only way to catch fish. We tried lures and caught a few fish, but most of the time we didn't catch anything on lures. This seemed to be the same for other people that we saw fishing with lures.

What is a Water-bubble?

A water-bubble is a round oval like clear bubble that you fill with water to allow baits to sink to the bottom. Now the reason I use this over snap weights is because the water-bubble allows the line to slide through it, so the fish won't feel any tension when it takes your bait. This will cause the fish to hold and eat your bait for longer allowing you to get a better hook set. It will also allow you to get a longer cast over snap weights.
