Strategies

Spring

In the spring the spoon fishing was very good at Lost Creek. The three best spoons to use where Kamloopers, Pot-O-Golds, and Crocodiles. Of the three the Kamloopers produced the most fish. The best colors to use where orange and gold or yellow and gold in the standard size, and red, white, and gold in the junior size. When retrieving the Kamloopers you would have to reel pretty slow, and sometimes extremely slow in order to get the fish to bite. The reason the extremely slow retrieve still worked with the Kamloopers is because you don't have to reel very fast in order for them to get some nice side-to-side action on them. Which was something the other two spoons didn't achieve as well. If the fish where a little more furious, which usually happened in the early mornings, then the other two spoons worked well along with the Kamloopers. The best Pot-O-Gold color to use was red and gold, and the best Crocodile color was silver. The Crocodile would really excel if the fish where very active, and hitting something that was moving fast because that is how you would get the best motion out of it. The Pot-O-Gold was the worst out of the three, but still worked very well. Using these spoons, we were able to catch 90 Trout in a morning of fishing.

If the spoon fishing ever died off, which sometimes happened in the late mornings, then fishing on the bottom was the next best thing. The best bait to use was half a worm and half a marshmallow on about a 1 to 3-foot leader with a standard sized water-bubble as the weight. If you don't know what a water-bubble is please read the paragraph at the bottom of the page. When it came to casting distance, it really depended on the day, so you would just have to experiment with different distances until you found the right distance. While bottom fishing worked well sometimes most of the time it was a very bad option due to the fact that most of the fish where on the surface, so we only used it as a last resort if the spoon bite was bad.

Summer

In the summer fishing from a boat was a better option then from shore due to the fact that the fish would be deeper and farther out because of the warmer temperatures. There were a few methods that worked. The first was jigging and trolling 2-inch tube jigs. The best colors to use where white and blue pearl pepper, which is more like a translucent blue. We also tipped our tube jigs with Chub meat, but you could use other baits and it would most likely work. We just strongly recommend that you tip your jig with something to give it a little more sent and flavor. Jigging worked best when it was calm. We recommend that you use electronics to find a good concentration of fish to give you the best odds. The best places to find large concentrations of fish were on ledges or steep drop-offs. If the wind picked up you could still jig, but it would be much harder to stay on top of the fish, or you could troll the tube jigs which worked but not as well as other methods when it was windy.

If the wind picked up and trolling tube jigs wasn't working, then trolling pop gear, dodgers, and triangle flashers with a worm would be the best option. The best pop gear was silver willow leaf, the best flasher color was silver, and With the dodger the best thing to do would be to make it a sling blade which is basically, bending the front of it more so that it has a very intense side-to-side action in the water. However, you don't want to have it spinning in the water if youâ€™re not reeling it. Depending on what time of summer you were up there would depend on how deep you would have to send your gear. In July most of the fish where 25-40 feet down, while in August most of them where 40-60 feet down. So, it would be very likely that you would have to use extra weight or down riggers to get your gear to the fish. We also recommend trolling at around 1 to 1.5 MPH. to get the most action. We also recommend trolling trough the same areas that you used the tube jigs because you already know there is a lot of fish there, and most likely there would be more fish in nearby areas. When it was windy this was the best method to use.

Another method that sometimes worked in the early mornings while it was calm would be casting small spoons like junior Kamloopers and Pot-O-Golds. The best Kamlooper color was red, white, and gold while the best Pot-O-Gold color was red and gold. This method rarely worked because the fish would rarely be on the surface, and if they where they wouldn't be chasing spoons very well but it was still a fun way to catch fish. It was just not going to produce near as much fish as the other two methods.

Fall

Fishing in November at Lost Creek can bring some great last-minute shore fishing before the lakes begin to freeze. The best methods to use where casting tube jigs, fishing on the bottom, and fishing with a bobber. When fishing with tube jigs you would want to use the same 2-inch white and blue pearl pepper jigs tipped with some bait, we still used Chub meat, that where mentioned in the summer portion. However, this time casting using a jig up and reel down method worked the best. For a video showing this casting method please refer to the bottom of the Strawberry report. When it came to how long you let your jig sink usually depended on the time of day. In the early mornings you wouldn't have to let you jig sink very far, but later in the day you would. This method worked best in early to mid-November. By late November this method pretty much didn't work at all.

Sometimes later in the day the tube jig fishing would really slow, so the next best method to use would be fishing on the bottom with half a worm and half a marshmallow on a 2 to 3-foot leader with a water-bubble as the weight. However, you would not want to cast your gear out very far because the fish would still be very shallow, sometimes to the point where you could just see them swimming by, because of the cooler water temperatures. Another great method to use if the tube jigs weren't working was fishing a bobber with a whole worm about 3-feet below the bobber. It was best to use the bobber if there was a bit of wind to get it to move and cover a larger area of water. These methods worked best in mid to late November when the water really started to get cold.

What is a Water-bubble?

A water-bubble is a round oval like clear bubble that you fill with water to allow baits to sink to the bottom. Now the reason I use this over snap weights is because the water-bubble allows the line to slide through it, so the fish won't feel any tension when it takes your bait. This will cause the fish to hold and eat your bait for longer allowing you to get a better hook set. It will also allow you to get a longer cast over snap weights.
