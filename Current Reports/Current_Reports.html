Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 1 month. After a lake or pond in the past reports reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Strawberry

Fishing for the most part is very fast. The only strange thing is that one day you can catch over 100 fish and the next struggle to catch 30 to 40 with the same weather conditions. Spoons are still the way to go. The best spoons now are large Pot-O-Golds with the best colors being: gold, silver, silver and green, gold and red/orange, and silver and orange. If smaller fish are following the larger Pot-O-Golds, but not biting, then it is best to use the next size down but still use the same color. Kamloopers also work well, the best colors for Kamloopers are: orange and gold, red and silver, and yellow and gold. Like the Pot-O-Golds if you are only getting follows on the jumbo Kamloopers then downsizing to a standard Kamlooper will be the best option. Another spoon that will sometimes work is a yellow Apex Five of Diamonds. If the spoons aren't working then Lucky Craft 115's or 2 to 3" white twisty tail or tube jigs tipped with Chub meat will also work. (Report from: 10/7/20)

Hobbs

Fishing is extremely slow for Trout. We tried multiple different spinners, spoons, and jigs and we only caught fish by bottom bouncing a small green and white Marabou. (Report from: 10/5/20)

Holmes

Water clarity is okay right now, and the fishing is hit and miss. Spoons and spinners are the best option. The best spinner is a sliver and green #2 Bluefox, but green Jake's will also sometimes work. The best spoon is a red and white junior Kamlooper, but fire tiger Daredevls and red Thunderbolt's will also work but they are a little big for the fish, so you might get some hits but have a hard time hooking up. (Report from: 9/30/20)

Adams

Due to the storm last weekend, the water clarity has really worsened. And because of this, the water is now a little warmer as well. While there are a lot of fish surfacing, because of the poor water clarity it is extremely hard to catch them on spinners and spoons. The spoons that worked were fire tiger or black and white Daredevls. We also tried tube jigs and saw people fly fishing and bottom fishing with little to no luck as well. Fishing should get better again once the water clears up and cools back down. (Report from: 9/23/20)
