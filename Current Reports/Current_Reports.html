Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 1 month. After a lake or pond in the past reports reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Strawberry

Fishing was very slow. We not only had a hard time catching fish, but we also had a hard time just seeing fish in general. The jigs that kind of worked the best were small white or green jigs. Other jigs that would also sometimes work were green and white or blue pearl pepper tube jigs. We tipped all our jigs with either a wax worm or Chub meat. (Report From: 3/2/21)

Jensen

The pond is ice free, and the fishing is good. The fish seem to be hitting best on the bottom right now. So, bottom fishing with bait is the best way to go. The bait that worked for us was a fourth to a half a worm with half a marshmallow on a 6 to 18" leader. We tried casting spinners and tube jigs as well as bottom bouncing the jigs and only got one bait using those methods. (Report From: 2/21/21)

Pineview

The ice is still plenty thick to walk on, around 10 to 12" not including the couple inches of slush on top of the ice. However, the edges are starting to get fairly soft, and to the point that you could break through in some areas later in the day. Fishing has slowed at little, but it is still fairly good. The fish seem to be around 40 feet of water now. The best lures to use are small green or fire tiger ice flies tipped with either Perch meat, a Perch eye, or wax worms. We have found that wax worms are the most consistent at getting bites. The fish are pretty much always going to be on the bottom. (Report From: 2/9/21)
