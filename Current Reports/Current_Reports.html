Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 1 month. After a lake or pond in the past reports reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Pineview

Fishing is really good right now for panfish. The best jigs to use are small yellow or red tube jigs tipped with either pink maggots or Perch meat. It is still possible to catch fish without tipping your jigs, but if you add Perch meat then the fish seem to bite more and faster. Most of the schools of fish are near the bottom right now. If school is Crappie then it is best to drop the jig to the bottom, then bring it up 2 to 3 feet and do a mixture of dead-sticking and jigging to get bites. If the school is Perch then dropping the jig to the bottom and then reeling till the line is tight and just waiting for the Perch to pick it up off the bottom is the best option. As for the Muskie fishing, it is very slow right now. We think this is mainly due to the lack of cover and structure in the water because the water level is a little lower right now. (Report from: 8/12/20)

Strawberry

Fishing is still really good. The fish now seem to be in large schools, so once you find them you are usually able to catch a lot in a short period of time. The best lure to use is 2.5 to 3.5" white tube jigs tipped with Chub meat. Because the fish are in larger schools, jigging is usually the best method to go to, but casting will also work well too. We also recommend just dropping a jig down as a dead stick to increase the odds of catching fish. This is also helpful if you want to cast your jig, but also still have a jigging rod out as well. The water temperature right now is in the low 70s. (Report from: 8/2/20)
