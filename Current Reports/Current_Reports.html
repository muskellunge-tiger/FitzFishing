Reports

Strawberry

Due to the storms and cooler temperatures the past couple days, the water temperature has dropped and now hangs around 64 to 65 degrees instead of 70. Fishing is still a little slower, but it has improved a bit from the cooler temperatures. 2.5 to 3" white tube jigs on a 3/8oz. jig head tipped with Chub meat is still the way to go. Casting and jigging both work well, but at times one will do better than the other. Most of the fish are shallower, but suspended in deeper water. It seemed that 60 to 65 feet was the best depth, and the fish would be hanging around 35 to 45 feet. (Report From: 8/20/21)

Lost Creek

The water level is really low, but the fishing is really good for Chub even from shore. You do not have to cast out far to catch a ton of Chub in a short amount of time. While this may not seem like the best quality fishing because it is Chub only it is still fun and if you use Chub for bait during other fishing trips, this is a great opportunity to get a lot of meat stocked up. The best way to catch them is to use either a 1 or 2 single hook rig with a small snap weight below both hooks for a little weight to cast tipped with a small piece of worm. After casting out let the rig sink to the bottom and if you're in the right area you should be getting hits within seconds. (Report From: 8/15/21)
