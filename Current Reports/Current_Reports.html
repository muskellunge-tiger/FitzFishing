Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 1 month. After a lake or pond in the past reports reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Jensen

Fishing is great. It seems a very good majority or fish are on the bottom right now, so that is the best way to fish. We used two different methods for fishing the bottom. The first, and most successful, was using half or a third of a worm and half a marshmallow on a 6 to 18" leader. The second, was bottom bounding a small pink and red tube jig tipped with a pink maggot. We also did see some fly fishermen doing well in the early morning when some of the fish were near the surface. (Report From: 3/18/21)

Kaysville

Fishing is pretty slow right now. There is almost no method that works consistently. We tried fishing multiple different jigs, on the bottom, and bobber and a worm. We only caught a few fish on a bobber and a worm, and couple fish on a few different jigs. Those being a small green tube jig, a small yellow Atomic tube, and a small white beetle jig with a black jig head. It seems that if there are clouds blocking the sun, it is extremely hard to almost impossible to catch any fish. (Report From: 3/14/21)

Strawberry

Fishing has improved a little bit. Smaller jigs are still the way to go most of the time. The best jigs are small yellow Atomic tubs, 2" white beetle jigs with a black jig head, and 2" green and white tube jigs. We tipped all our jigs with either Chub meat or wax worms. It seemed that the fish were shallower in the morning and deeper in the afternoon. We were in about 45 feet in the morning and moved to about 55 feet in the afternoon. (Report From: 3/11/21)
