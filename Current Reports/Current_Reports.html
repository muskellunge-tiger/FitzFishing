Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 1 month. After a lake or pond in the past reports reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Pineview

The Muskie fishing is still very slow. We were able to see some Muskie swimming along the surface, but weren't able to catch them. While the Crappie fishing seems to have slowed, the Perch fishing is red hot. The best lure to use is a small yellow Atomic tube jig tipped with Perch meat. The best depth range to target is around 12 to 16 feet. Most of the time the Perch will either be on the bottom or very close to it. Because of this, you are also able to catch quite a few Catfish. (Report from: 9/22/20)

Hobbs

The fishing has really slowed over the past few days. It seems most of the Trout are now near the bottom, and are not hitting spinners and spoons near as good as they were. It is still possible to catch them on silver or cooper Pot-O-Gold's, but pretty much every other spinner or spoon didn't work. (Report from: 9/21/20)

Adams

Fishing is fair to slow. The water clarity is pretty good considering how warm it has been. There are a lot of fish surfacing, so spinners and spoons are the way to go. The best spoons are fire tiger Daredevls and yellow and gold Kamloopers. The best spinner is a silver and green number 2 Bluefox. Some fish are very close to shore, so making shoreline casts is usually the best way to go. While the fishing may not be as good right now, we believe that it will get much better in the coming weeks. (Report from: 9/15/20)

Strawberry

Fishing is pretty good right now. The water temperature in the morning is around 55 degrees and it's around 60 degrees by the afternoon. Spoons seem to be the best lures to use. The best spoons to use are jumbo gold and red Kamloopers or gold Pot-O-Gold's. A slower retrieve is best right now as the fish are not quite in full fall feeding mode. The best depths to fish are around 40 to 45 feet, but if you see fish surfacing in an area then it is best to fish that area instead. (Report from: 9/13/20)
