Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 1 month. After a lake or pond in the past reports reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Strawberry

The ice is still plenty thick to walk on. However, on warmer afternoons, the top does start to melt so it does get a little wet, but it's nothing too serious right now. Fishing is still good, and smaller jigs are still the way to go. The jig that dominated for us was a 2" white beetle jig with a black jig head tipped with either wax worm or Chub meat. Yellow Atomic tubes and small white Trout magnet jigs also worked well for fish that were higher in the water (15 feet or less). We also found that if you are having trouble seeing fish, to either move or consistently jig your jig to bring fish in. (Report From: 4/1/21)

Holmes

Water clarity is pretty good and the water temperature is cold as well. Fishing is slow right now, but because there was a recent stocking, we are hoping that the fishing will get better once the water warms up a little more. The best lures for us were a red, white, and gold junior Kamlooper and a #2 silver and green Bluefox. We also had some bites and follows on a lil green Jake and some small tube jigs. We also tried flies, but with no success. (Report From: 3/31/21)
