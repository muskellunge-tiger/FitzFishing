Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 1 month. After a lake or pond in the past reports reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Kaysville

Catfish fishing is very good right now. The best bait to use is worm and half a marshmallow. Because the fish tend to be very small, it is best to only use a fourth of a worm for bait and a smaller hook, so the fish can get it in there mouth easier. (Report from: 7/14/20)

Lost Creek

Fishing is fairly good right now, but mainly in morning. By the afternoon the bite really dies off. The best methods to use are trolling and jigging. When trolling, silver willow leaf or tomahawk bladed pop gear with a worm works the best. When jigging, smaller tube jigs work better. 1.5-2" skinny white and green tube jigs work best. Most of the fish we caught were around 11 to 14 inches. (Report from: 7/8/20)

Strawberry

Fishing is very good right now. The water temperature is mainly in the low 60's. The best method is casting or jigging a 2" white tube jig tipped with Chub meat. Spoons will also sometimes work as well. The best spoons to use are yellow and gold or orange and gold Kamloopers. The best depths to target are 40 to 60 feet. (Report from: 7/3/20)

East Canyon

Fishing is extremely slow right now. The water temperature is 66 to 71 degrees depending on the time of day. Most of the fish seem to be really spread out, and if you do find a small group of fish, they tend to not bite anything. We tried multiple different lures and methods from trolling to tube jigs to crankbaits and spoons. We only caught fish while trolling silver willow leaf or tomahawk pop gear and a worm and a small white and green Marabou. (Report from: 6/24/20)
