Strategies

After a slow start in early spring Holmes fishing got really good. The main reason for this was because of a few stockings of around 1,700 fish, and very good water clarity. The best method to use was either fishing a small spoon or fly fishing. The only spoon we found to work very well was a red, white, and gold junior Kamlooper. However, small Pot-O-Golds and small silver lipless crankbaits would sometimes catch fish. When fishing with these lures a slower retrieve was the way to go. For example, you would only want the small spoons to swing side to side instead of spin. We also found that the fishing was best when the weather was horrible. If it was raining or snowing that was when the fish seemed to be the most active on the surface. If you were fly fishing you would need to cast your fly out a long way, probably around 40-50 feet, or fish in a non-motorized boat in order to reach the fish. Bottom fishing also worked quite well, just not as well as the spoons or the flies.

When it came to the fall the water clarity had gotten bad, and the bite had really slowed. The fish were not hitting spoons or flies very well at all. Most of the fish that were in close were small Bluegill and Bass while the Trout were out in the deeper water. We never really fished Holmes in the fall due to the success that Adams had brought.
