Strategies

In late spring Hobbs was stocked with 13-inch Cutthroat Trout. This caused the fishing to be excellent for a few weeks. Fishing with spinners and spoons in the mornings and evenings was a great way to catch some decent sized Cutthroats. The best spinner to use was a silver Jake while the best spoons to use were a silver Crocodile or a silver and blue Kastmaster. However, orange and gold Kamloopers would also work, but not as well because they dove to deep. When it came to retrieving the spoons and spinners, a slower retrieve worked well for the Jake because that is how you got the maximum action out of it. A fast retrieve was good for the Crocodile and the Kastmaster.

Unfortunately, because this happened in late April into early May the bite did not last that long, as summer was coming in quick, and the water temperature began to rise quick and the fish moved out of range for shore fishing. We didn't fish Hobbs much in the fall due to the success that Adams had brought.
