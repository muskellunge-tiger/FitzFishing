Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 4 weeks. After a lake or pond reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Rocport

Water temperature is in the high 60s, and the fishing is fairly slow except for the early mornings. Casting and trolling yellow and gold Kamloopers is a good method early on when the fish are near the surface. In the late morning trolling silver willow leaf pop gear with a worm around 20-25 feet down at around 1.0 to 1.5 mph. is the best option. Trolling silver triangle flashers with a worm at the same depth is another good option. (Report from: 7/15/19)

Strawberry

Water temperature is in the low 60s. Fishing has slowed a little when casting white tube jigs, but has gotten much faster jigging them. We recommend using 2" jigs tipped with Chub meat. Casting red and gold, and yellow and gold Kamloopers got a lot of bites. You may have to let the Kamlooper sink a little, but most of the time fish will be on the surface. When trolling for Kokanee, we found that using silver willow leaf and tomahawk pop gear with wedding rings and squids tipped with pink maggots would do pretty good. Just remember to try different depths till you where the fish are. (Report from: 7/2/19)
