Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 4 weeks. After a lake or pond reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Holmes

While there is a fair amount of fish in the lake that are in close and hitting the surface, the fishing is still a little slow. Spinners and spoons are quite doing good yet, and tube jigs are also not performing well either. The only success that we had was fishing with woolly bugger flies. We also some other people catch a few fish off the bottom using worms. But mainly for right now it seems as if the fishing is still slow. (Report from: 3/26/20)

Adams

Fishing is very slow right now with spoons and spinners. We tried multiple different spoons and spinners. Along with multiple different colors, with only a few that worked. The best spoons are a red white and gold junior Kamlooper and white Pot-O-Golds. The best spinner is a green Jake, but all in all none of these lures really perform better than the other. (Report from: 3/22/20)

Strawberry

There is pretty much no more slush on the lake and the ice is about 20" thick. The fishing however, has slowed massively. While at lot of fish would come through they would not bite very often at all. We believe this is because most of the Cutthroats that would come through were very small, and who not be able to eat bigger jigs, and then they would not be interested in smaller jigs either. The best way to catch fish would be to drop a small yellow Atomic tube down about 15 feet down and wait for either Rainbows or Kokanee to come through. This is because the Rainbows and Kokanee are bigger and much more aggressive then the Cutts right now. However, this problem will likely change very soon. (Report from: 3/16/20)

Hobbs

The lake just recentally unfroze, so the fishing is a little slow right now. However, there appears to be quite a few fish in the lake, so the fishing should pick up in the coming weeks. The best lures that worked for us were fire tiger Daredevils and silver and green number 2 Bluefoxes. A slower retrieve is better right now because the water is still a little cold. (Report from: 3/15/20)
