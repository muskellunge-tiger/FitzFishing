Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 4 weeks. After a lake or pond reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Lost Creek

Fishing is still decent from the shore. There is a lot of fish up shallow in the morning, but then they go deeper in the early afternoon. Fishing 2-2.5 inch tube jigs, preferably white or translucent blue pepper tipped with Chub meat or worm, is a great way to catch fish. Fishing on the bottom with half a worm and half a marshmallow is also a good way to catch fish. Just remember to cast your bottom gear shorter in the morning, but farther in the afternoon. Fishing a bobber with a whole worm on about a 3-foot leader is also a great way to catch fish. Most of the fish are around 14 to 15 inches. (Report from: 11/17/19)

Good Ski

Fishing is great with tube jigs. You will want to use 1.5" orange, yellow, or white tube jigs tipped with a pink maggot. You also want to use a lighter jig head with a longer hook, so it sinks slower and so you have a better hookup chance. The best method to catch them is using a bottom bouncing method. Most of the fish are around 12", but there is plenty of 13-15 inchers as well. (Report from: 11/15/19)

Strawberry

Water temperature is around 40 degrees, and fishing is fast. The best lures to use are 2.5 to 4" tube jigs. The best color for us was white. We recommend that you tip your tube jigs with Chub meat. The best methods to use are jigging and casting using a jig up and reel down method. Most of the fish are in shallow water, but there are some deeper fish. A good depth to look for is 40 feet. Most of the fish are around 17 inches. (Report from: 11/9/19)
