Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 4 weeks. After a lake or pond reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Strawberry

Fishing is fast when it is sunny, but much slower when cloudy. Water temperature is in the high 60s. Most of the fish are around 35 to 45 feet down. Casting and jigging 2-3" white tube jigs with 3/8 oz. jig head tipped with Chub meat will catch a ton of fish. Usually while it is calm casting works better, and while it is windy jigging works better. You may also be able to catch a few fish on a spinner or spoon on calm sunny mornings. (Report from: 7/31/19)

Lost Creek

Fishing is good from a boat in most of the lake. Trolling and jigging tube jigs will catch you a good amount of fish. While it is clam find an area with a far number of fish, and jig 1.5 to 2 inch white and translucent blue tube jigs tipped with Chub meat. When the wind picks up troll silver willow leaf pop gear, sliver triangle flashers, or sling rig dodgers with a worm at around 25-40 feet depending on the time of day. (Report from: 7/25/19)

East Canyon

Fishing has really slowed. Trolling pop gear and a worm around 25-30 feet down in the early morning is the best way to catch a few fish. By late morning and early afternoon the bite really slows. (Report from: 7/24/19)
