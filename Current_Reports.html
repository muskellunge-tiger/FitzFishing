Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 4 weeks. After a lake or pond reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Tropic

The is about as full as it can get, and has a ton of little Rainbows, Tigers, Browns. The water is very clear, so it is perfect for spinner and spoon fishing. However, even though you can get a ton of followers the fish just almost never bite. Because if this the fishing is very slow. (Report from: 6/18/19)

Rockport

Fishing is good for Trout on the south end of the lake, but very slow on the north end. Trolling silver and gold 4 to 6 blade willow leaf pop gear at around 1 mph. 100 feet behind the boat does pretty good. If you see a decent number of fish breaking the surface then casting perch pattern spoons can get you a few extra bites. The water temp is in the high 50s. Most of the fish are around 15 inches. (Report from: 6/5/19)

East Canyon

The reservoir is full and the fishing is hot. Water temp in the morning is around 55 degrees, and warms up to around 59 degrees in the afternoon. Trolling pop gear without weight 100 feet behind the boat at around 1 mph. is the best way to catch fish right now. The best pop gear for us was, 4 bladed silver or gold willow leaf. Triangle gold, silver, and green flashers also worked. We tried casting spoons, crankbaits, and jigs but had no luck. Most of the fish where 13 to 16 inches with a few smaller and bigger ones. (Report from: 6/3/19)
