Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 4 weeks. After a lake or pond reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Adams

Fishing is still pretty fast, but only when it is sunny. Fishing bigger spoons is the way to go. The best spoon to use is a yellow and gold Kamlooper. However, other spoons like rainbow patterned Daredevils and junior Kamloopers also work, just not as well as the yellow Kamlooper. Most of the fish are around 10", but they can be as big as 15" and there is the occasional Wiper mixed in with the Trout. (Report from: 10/23/19)

East Canyon

Fishing is decent and there is not much algae in the water as long as you stay away from the dam. While there is a lot of activity on the surface, most of the fish are not hitting spoons or spinners. Trolling pop gear and a worm will catch a few fish. However, jigging small green and white tube jigs tipped with pink maggots or worm will catch a lot of fish very fast as long as you are over a lot of fish. The best way to find an area with a lot fish in it is to use your electronics to find them and the depth they are at. (Report from: 10/18/19)

Strawberry

The water temperature is 52 degrees, and there is quite a bit of algae throughout the lake. However, this is not hindering the fish because the bit is hot. The lures to use are big spoons and big jerkbaits or minnowbaits. The best color for spoons is orange, but other colors like yellow, red, or rainbow pattern also work. Lucky Craft 115s that are patterned like Chub will get you a lot of bigger fish. The best way to trigger a bite with the Lucky Crafts is to use a stop and go retrieve. Most of the fish are around 12-14 inches, but there is plenty of 16-18 inchers and occasionally there is a slot buster within those smaller fish. (Report from: 10/5/19)
