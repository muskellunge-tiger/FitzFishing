Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 4 weeks. After a lake or pond reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Kaysville

The ponds are free of ice, and fishing is great. Pretty much any method works whether it be casting jigs, fishing on the bottom, fly fishing, etc. you have a good chance of getting a fish. We found that fishing a small green tube jig tipped with Berkley Gulp pink maggots worked well when casting with a slow retrieve. Bottom fishing half a worm and marshmallow also worked great. Most of the trout are around 10-12", with the occasional 20 incher.

Strawberry

Between the ice and snow there is around 2 feet before you get to the water. Fish mainly come through at around 35-40 feet of water with the occasional high flyer about 15-20 feet below the ice. Fishing dodgers with jig around 1.5-2 feet below the dodger is a great way to catch and bring fish in. Smaller tube jigs also work well with the best colors being green or white. Tipping your jigs with Chub meat or worms will up your chances at getting a better bite. (Report from: 2/26/19)

Jensen

Jensen is completely clear of ice. Fishing is still kind of slow, but is getting better now that the pond is unfrozen. Fishing on the bottom with half a marshmallow and corn was the best method. We only saw people catching fish on the bottom, and none when casting lures. We did get a few hits on lures, but the bottom fishing was a lot better. (Report from: 2/17/19)
