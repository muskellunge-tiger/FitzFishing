Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 4 weeks. After a lake or pond reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Jensen

Fishing is really good right now; most methods work as long as they don't require a fast retrieve because it is still a little too cold for spoons and spinners to work. However, this does not mean that they won't work. It just means that they won't work as well as other methods at this time. The best method to use is bottom fishing either half a worm and half a marshmallow, chartreuse green powerbait, rainbow Trout nuggets, or just a whole marshmallow. The leader length that works the best for us is a 6" leader, but a longer leader will likely work too. Another method that works is either slowly reeling in or bottom bouncing tube jigs or Marabou's. The best color for tube jigs is orange or white, and the best Marabou color for us was a white and green Marabou. We recommend that you tip your jigs with some sort of bait. The bait we used was pink maggots, but worms or corn will also likely work. (Report from: 2/2/20)

Strawberry

While there is a fair amount of slush on the lake, most of it has hardened, but slush conditions constantly change so this may not be the case in a day or two. As the fishing goes, it has slowed a little bit. The best depth for us was around 35 to 45 feet. The best jig to use for deeper fish is a 2 to 3-inch white tube jig tipped with Chub. We also recommend that you add a "stinger hook" to your tube jig because the fish tend to just nip at the tails of the jigs, so this will give you a better chance at hooking up. For higher flying fish, it is best to use Marabou's. The specific color that we used was orange, but we have had success with green and white as well. (Report from: 2/1/20)
