Welcome to the Current Reports page. This page will provide fishing reports from recent trips that we have made to lakes and ponds. There will be up to 4 reports provided on this page. If you would like to see past reports for individual lakes and ponds please go to the Past Reports page. A report will be removed from this page regardless of the circumstances after 3 weeks, and reports from the past reports page will be removed after 4 weeks. After a lake or pond reaches more than 3 reports the oldest report will be removed regardless of the time it has been up. There will be no duplicated lakes on the Current Reports part, but you can find the others in the Past Reports. Please know that the rate in which new reports are entered may very depending on the time of year.

Reports

Kaysville

Due to a recent stocking of Trout, the fishing is very good. The two best methods to use are fishing on the bottom with half a worm and half a marshmallow and fishing with a small yellow Atomic tube tipped with a pink maggot. Another method that kind of works is fishing with a red gold and white junior Kamlooper, but because it is still a little early in the year it is not the best option at this time. When fishing with the tube jig you either want to retrieve it slowly or cast it out and let it rest on the bottom. However, if you don't get a bite within a minute or two then slowly retrieve it and cast it back out. If you find an area with a lot of fish in it then this bottom tube jig fishing with get you a bite pretty much every cast. (Report from: 3/5/20)

Strawberry

There is very little slush on the lake, and only a couple of inches of snow. The fishing is pretty good right now, in about 50 to 60 feet of water. The best lures to use are 2 to 3" white tube jigs and orange or white Marabou's. When using the white tube jigs we recommend that you add a "stinger hook" to it because of the fact that the fish sometimes nip at the tail of the jig. We also recommend that you tip all your jigs with some sort of bait. The bait we used was a piece of Chub meat. (Report from: 3/3/20)

Good Ski Lake

Fishing is starting to pick up even more now that the pond has been unfrozen for a couple more days. There are 2 really good methods to use they are: bottom fishing and fishing with 1.5-2” tube jigs or 1” Marabou's. When bottom fishing the best bait to use is half a worm and half a marshmallow on about a 6" leader. When fishing tube jigs the best colors to use are yellow and orange, but other colors like green and white will also likely work. When using Marabou’s, the best color is green and white. We recommend that you tip you jigs with some kind of bait, we used pink maggots, to increase bites. When fishing the jigs, it is best to either bottom bounce or cast them out about 15 to 20 feet and just let it sit on the bottom. If you are in an area that has a lot of fish then this method will sometimes get you bites the second the jig hits the bottom. If that doesn't happen then within about a minute you will get bit. If your jig is sitting for more than a couple of minutes without a bite then recast it in a different area or try bottom bouncing to cover more area till you find them. (Report from: 2/26/20)
