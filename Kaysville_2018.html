Strategies

Winter

Because it was so warm in 2018 it made it so Kaysville didn't really ever freeze which allowed for some awesome winter fishing. The best stratege in January was fishing small 1" tube jigs, sometimes tipped with worms, very slowly. What you would want to do is cast out as far as you could, and reel back slowly. The reason you would have to reel back slow is due to the very dirty water that is commonly at Kaysville. The best color was green as it is bright enough to be seen in the dirty water.

Once you got into February all of the trout stopped feeding on the surface, and went to the bottom. When this happened bait fishing the bottom was then the key to success. What you would want to do is fish half a worm and half a marshmallow off the bottom with a water-bubble with about a foot to foot and a half leader. If you don't know what a water-bubble is please read the paragraph below. Sometimes if you where lucky the trout would be very agressive on the bottom, and you would only have to fish a mashmallow off the bottom which would really speed your catching.

Spring

By the time March came around it had warmed up even more to very nice spring tempuratures. This caused all the trout to come back to the surface. This meant that you would want to use the stratege that was use in January again. But this time by you almost never had to tip you jig with worms as the fish were very agressive. Another method that worked was fishing half a worm on a bobber, with the worm around a foot and a half below the surface. When using these methods in March shortly after the pond had been stocked with some big Rainbow Trout we cuaght 53 fish in one day which was our highest number day in 2018.

Summer

When fishing Kaysville in the summer months it is all about fishing for Catfish. The best way to catch these things is to fishing half a worm and half a marshmallow on a water-bubble off the bottom just like in February. Using this method you will mainly catch Bullhead Catfish which aren't usually that big, but you can sometimes catch some 20+ inch Channel Catfish which for Kaysville is very nice catch. This method can also catch grass carp. The best time to use this method is shortly after the pond is stcoked with Channel Catfish. The best way to check for when this happens is to check the Utah Fish Stocking Report which is linked in the footer.

It is this time of year at Kaysville that I think is the best time for new fishermen, but mainly children that are just learning how to fish as you can get some reasonably fast action with the potential of catching a very nice sized Channel Catfish. With the right gear this will put a great fight, but not break your line. If you use 8-10 pound line you will be more then fine in almost every situation.

What is a Water-bubble?

A water-bubble is a round oval like clear bubble that you fill with water to allow baits to sink to the bottom. Now the reason I use this over snap weights is because the water-bubble allows the line to slide through it also the fish won't feel any tension when it takes you bait. This will cause the fish to hold and eat your bait for longer allowing you to get a better hook set.
