Welcome to the Species Effectiveness page. On this page I will go over different species of fish, and based on my opinion and experiences rate a tube jigs effectiveness when fishing for that species. The breakdowns will include: the effectiveness rating, best and worst times of the year to use tube jigs for them, and a short reasoning about the data. Again, please note that all of this data is based on my own opinion and experiences. Also note that what I say here is the most likely case, but as with a lot of things there may be exceptions.

Species

Trout

  • Effectiveness Rating = 9/10
  • Best Times = Early Spring, Summer, Late Fall, Winter
  • Worst Times = Late Spring and Early to Late Fall

In my honest opinion I believe that Trout are the best species to target when fishing with tube jigs. This is because most Trout species are predatory and hunt small bait fish which is what a tube jig mimics. The reason that late spring and early to late fall is because this is when the water tempuratures are a the right for other lures like spinners and spoons which is a much faster presentation. Whereas in early spring, late fall, and winter the water tempurature is much cooler, so a slower presentation is much better.
