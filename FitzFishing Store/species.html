Welcome to the Species Effectiveness page. On this page I will go over different species of fish, and based on my opinion and experiences rate a tube jigs effectiveness when fishing for that species. The breakdowns will include: the effectiveness rating, best and worst times of the year to use tube jigs for them, and a short reasoning about the data. Again, please note that all of this data is based on my own opinion and experiences. Also note that what I say here is the most likely case, but as with a lot of things there may be exceptions.

Species

Trout

  • Effectiveness Rating = 9/10
  • Best Times = Early Spring, Summer, Late Fall, Winter
  • Worst Times = Late Spring and Early to Late Fall

In my honest opinion I believe that Trout are the best species to target when fishing with tube jigs. This is because most Trout species are predatory and hunt small baitfish which is what a tube jig mimics. The reason that late spring and early to late fall is the worst time of year is because this is when the water temperatures are perfect for other lures like spinners and spoons which have a much faster presentation. Whereas in early spring, late fall, and winter the water temperature is much cooler, so a slower presentation is much better.

Muskie

  • Effectiveness Rating = 1/10
  • Best Times = Pretty much never
  • Worst Times = Pretty much always

While Muskie is my favorite species of fish to fish for, using tube jigs to catch them is just not a good option. The reason is because you need to mimic baitfish very well to get them to strike, and while tube jigs are supposed to mimic bait fish, they don't do it good enough to convince a Muskie to bite most of the time.

Panfish

  • Effectiveness Rating = 8/10
  • Best Times = When the fish are in large schools
  • Worst Times = When the fish are not in large schools

Of all the species listed on this page, this is probably the species that will break the best and worst times of the year the most. This is simply because small panfish like Crappie, Perch, and Bluegill are almost always hunting for small baitfish which a tube jig mimics well. So, when I say the best time of the year to use tube jigs for them is when the fish are in large schools, petty much means that it is the best time to catch a large number of them in a short amount of time. I know this is true because my dad and I have caught 104 Crappie in a large school in 90 minutes of fishing.

Catfish

  • Effectiveness Rating = 1/10
  • Best Times = Pretty much never
  • Worst Times = Pretty much always

In my opinion, fishing exclusively for Catfish with tube jigs is just a terrible idea. The reason is because Catfish are almost always glued to the bottom looking for live bait and causations. They are also usually quite lethargic and don't tend to chase after lures like tube jigs. However, there is always exceptions because there are people who have caught very large Catfish on jigs, crankbaits, and spinners, but those people where fishing for other species and just happened to have a Catfish attack their lure.

Kokanee Salmon

  • Effectiveness Rating = 5/10
  • Best Times = Summer and somtimes Winter
  • Worst Times = Fall and Spring

When using tube jigs for Kokanee or any Salmon you have to remember that this is only going to work in lakes or the ocean, not in rivers. If you are fishing for them in rivers then a fly will be your best option. If you are fishing for them in a lake in the summer then the best thing to do is use a tube jig or squid like jig while trolling with either pop gear or a dodger. When it comes to actually jigging for them, it is rare that you will be able to jig for them in the summer, all though there are exceptions. The best time to jig for them is in the winter when you are ice fishing. While this is not the most effective method for beginners, if you are able to figure out where the Salmon are at most of the time then you can catch a ton of them very fast due to the fact that they are almost always in large schools.
