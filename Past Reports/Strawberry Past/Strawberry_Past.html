Strawberry Past Reports

The water temperature is 55 to 60 degrees depending on the time of day and where you are on the lake. The summer to fall transition seems to be in full swing right now, which means fishing is a little tough because a lot of the fish are spread out in many different areas and depths. For us, the best depth seemed to be around 50'. The best methods continue to be tube jigs and spoons. The best tube jig continues to be 2.5 to 3" white tube jig tipped with Chub meat. The best spoons are jumbo orange and gold Kamloopers and yellow Apex Five of Diamonds, but large gold Pot-O-Golds, and standard size Kamloopers will also work. In some cases, Lucky Craft 115's work as well using a fast stop and go retrieve. (Report From: 9/21/21)

Water temperature is about 62 to 63 degrees in the morning and around 65 to 66 degrees in the afternoon. Most of the fish are still in a summer to fall transition, but they are beginning to move up into shallower water and higher in the water column now that the temperatures are dropping. Most of the fish are 20 to 35' down and suspended in about 50 to 55' most of the time. 2.5 to 3" white tube jigs tipped with Chub meat continue to work very well, but spoons are also beginning to work as well. The best spoons for us were standard size Kamloopers that had gold as some part of their coloring. The best Kamlooper was a standard size orange and gold. We expect that in the next couple weeks the spoon fishing will increase a lot when the fish get into full fall feeding. (Report From: 9/14/21)

Water temperature has not really changed recently, and is still around 64 to 65 degrees. Fishing has continued to slow a little as we are now in the summer to fall transition. Most of the fish right now are suspended in about 65 to 70' and around 35 to 40 down. 2.5 to 3" white tube jigs tipped with Chub meat continue to be the best option. (Report From: 8/26/21)
