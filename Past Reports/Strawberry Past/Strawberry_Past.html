Strawberry Past Reports

The ice is still plenty thick to walk on. However, on warmer afternoons, the top does start to melt so it does get a little wet, but it's nothing too serious right now. Fishing is still good, and smaller jigs are still the way to go. The jig that dominated for us was a 2" white beetle jig with a black jig head tipped with either wax worm or Chub meat. Yellow Atomic tubes and small white Trout magnet jigs also worked well for fish that were higher in the water (15 feet or less). We also found that if you are having trouble seeing fish, to either move or consistently jig your jig to bring fish in. (Report From: 4/1/21)

Fishing has improved a little bit. Smaller jigs are still the way to go most of the time. The best jigs are small yellow Atomic tubs, 2" white beetle jigs with a black jig head, and 2" green and white tube jigs. We tipped all our jigs with either Chub meat or wax worms. It seemed that the fish were shallower in the morning and deeper in the afternoon. We were in about 45 feet in the morning and moved to about 55 feet in the afternoon. (Report From: 3/11/21)

Fishing was very slow. We not only had a hard time catching fish, but we also had a hard time just seeing fish in general. The jigs that kind of worked the best were small white or green jigs. Other jigs that would also sometimes work were green and white or blue pearl pepper tube jigs. We tipped all our jigs with either a wax worm or Chub meat. (Report From: 3/2/21)
