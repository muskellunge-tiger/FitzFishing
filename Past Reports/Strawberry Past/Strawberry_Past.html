Bigger tube jigs don't tend to work as well because the fish seem to not be after larger bait right now. We recommend that you tip your jigs with either nightcrawlers, wax worms, or Chub meat. While most of the fish are around 15 to 40 feet of water, you want to fish in water that is a little bit deeper, like 60+ feet. Most of the fish are around 15". (Report from: 3/29/20)

There is pretty much no more slush on the lake and the ice is about 20" thick. The fishing however, has slowed massively. While at lot of fish would come through they would not bite very often at all. We believe this is because most of the Cutthroats that would come through were very small, and who not be able to eat bigger jigs, and then they would not be interested in smaller jigs either. The best way to catch fish would be to drop a small yellow Atomic tube down about 15 feet down and wait for either Rainbows or Kokanee to come through. This is because the Rainbows and Kokanee are bigger and much more aggressive then the Cutts right now. However, this problem will likely change very soon. (Report from: 3/16/20)

There is very little slush on the lake, and only a couple of inches of snow. The fishing is pretty good right now, in about 50 to 60 feet of water. The best lures to use are 2 to 3" white tube jigs and orange or white Marabou's. When using the white tube jigs we recommend that you add a "stinger hook" to it because of the fact that the fish sometimes nip at the tail of the jig. We also recommend that you tip all your jigs with some sort of bait. The bait we used was a piece of Chub meat. (Report from: 3/3/20)
