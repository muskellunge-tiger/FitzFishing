Report and Stratagies

Stratagies

When fishing Adams there are a few conditions to look for that will make your fishing experence amazing. First, if your fishing with spinners or anything that is going to be near the surface you want to be there on a day is not very windy. The reason is because the trout tend to feed on the surface when it is calm. Second, clear skies will help you in almost any circumstance, but it will help most when fishing fast which seems to be the best stratagy at Adams. Now this does not mean that if it is cloudy that you won't catch fish. It just means that you may not catch as many fish. Third, if you are fishing fast, like with spinners or cranks, water clearitiy is going to be the biggest factor when it comes to your success. If you do run into a situation where the water is a little cloudy you can still fish fast just simiply use a brighter bait like green or red, and maybe slow your reel down. Finally, if you see fish jumping like crazy in a spot that nobody is already at move to that spot as fast as possible. I have see a ton of times that if you go to where the fish are jumping you will catch a ton of them, up to 30 or more, in a short peroid of time long as you use the right gear.

Spring

Fishing Adams in the spring is a very easy task as long as you follow the few factors dicussed above, and have the correct gear with you. When spring fishing at Adams we found that if it was a sunnny day with clear water the best lure to use was a little silver jake. On these days all you would have to do is cast it as far as you could, and just reel back at a reasonably fast speed and just wait to get rocked! Alonge with the silver jake, a red jake was also a great option especially if was cloudy or around sunset as there was lower light making a bright jake a great option as well. Another great lure to use where crankbaits. These things where great in the early evening on very sunny days. They also tended to catch biggest fish at times, and some people where also able to catch multiple Wipers with these. Some retreve stragies for crankbaits where what I call hard twitch wait about a second reel hard twitch repeat. This method worked great for catching Trout, and where most likely to hit after shortly after the first twitch. Another method was just hard twitch after hard twitch, but it didn't seem to work as well as the previous method. The final method was just cast and reel back at a decent speed. This method seemed to work the best out of the three, and was the way some people where catching Wipers. Unfortunally we where unable to catch a Wiper, but we did see others catching them. All in all Adams in the spring was a great place to fish as we where able to catch over a 100 fish in around ten trips with a high of 42 in just a few hours. I strongly recomend this place for people who are learning how to fish as you can catch a lot of Rainbow Trout in short amount of time, and the best thing about the rainbows is that they will put up a great fight, but the don't fight so hard that they are constintally breaking you off. Please know that if you are over the age of 14 you are required the have an Access Permit, which is free, as well as a valid fishing license.

Fall

When it came to fishing Adams in the fall it just plan sucked, and the biggest reason for this was do to the unusually hot summer that Utah had which caused Adams water level to drop a ton. This caused the water clearitiy to really drop making it impossible to fish with spinnes and have any sort of success. But in October there where a few good days in which the same method from the spring worked. However, unlike the spring crankbaits did not work, and silver jakes where a horrible option do to the water. The best lures to use where little green and red jakes with a slitly slower retreve. Behond that it was very hard to fishing Adams this fall.
