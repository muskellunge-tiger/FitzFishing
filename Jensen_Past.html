Jensen Past Reports

Fishing is really good right now; most methods work as long as they don't require a fast retrieve because it is still a little too cold for spoons and spinners to work. However, this does not mean that they won't work. It just means that they won't work as well as other methods at this time. The best method to use is bottom fishing either half a worm and half a marshmallow, chartreuse green powerbait, rainbow Trout nuggets, or just a whole marshmallow. The leader length that works the best for us is a 6" leader, but a longer leader will likely work too. Another method that works is either slowly reeling in or bottom bouncing tube jigs or Marabou's. The best color for tube jigs is orange or white, and the best Marabou color for us was a white and green Marabou. We recommend that you tip your jigs with some sort of bait. The bait we used was pink maggots, but worms or corn will also likely work. (Report from: 2/2/20)

The pond is completely ice free, and the fishing is pretty good. The best method to use is fishing on the bottom with either half a worm and half a marshmallow or a whole rainbow patterned Trout nugget. However, we recommend that you cut the Trout nugget in half then put it on the hook, so that it will stay on the hook better and give it a more compact look. This compact look is good because most of the fish are smaller. Another method the works is bottom bouncing a 1.5" yellow tube jig tipped with a piece of fire corn (pink piece of corn). (Report from: 1/25/20)

The pond is pretty much unfrozen, and the fishing is slow. The best and only method we found to work was fishing on the bottom with half a worm and half a marshmallow on about a 6" leader. We tried fishing small tube jigs tipped with pink maggots and a bobber with a worm, but were unable to catch any fish. Pretty much all of the fish are very small. (Report from: 1/18/20)
